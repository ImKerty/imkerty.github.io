TCP/IP, which stands for Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol, is a set of communication protocols that form the backbone of the Internet. It is a fundamental suite of protocols governing the way data is sent, received, and routed across networks.


TCP/IP plays a crucial role in enabling communication and data transfer between devices connected to the Internet. It provides a standardized method for breaking down data into packets, transmitting them across networks, and reassembling them at their destination.
