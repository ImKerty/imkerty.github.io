1960s

Originating with the ARPANET project, the first network to use packet-switching technology.

Early 1970s

Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn developed the Transmission Control Program (TCP) as part of ARPANET, marking the conceptual beginning of TCP/IP.

1973

Cerf and Kahn published a paper outlining the foundational principles of TCP.

Late 1970s

The original TCP split into Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and Internet Protocol (IP) for enhanced modularity.

1980s

TCP/IP became the standard for network protocols, extending to commercial and public networks. The Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) played a crucial role in standardization.

1983s

TCP/IP adopted as the standard for ARPANET, leading to global adoption.

1990s

TCP/IP supported the commercialization of the Internet and the rise of the World Wide Web.

2000s to Present

TCP/IP remains the dominant protocol suite, contributing to the continuous growth and evolution of the digital landscape
